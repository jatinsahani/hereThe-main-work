Solo Travel Destinations — “Best places to travel alone in Southeast Asia”

Take a break then leave your desk, pack your bag and, see the different world!

Tired of a busy life?

Take a break then leave your desk, pack your bag and, see the different world!


Solo travel is a great way to explore new places and meet new people. When you’re traveling alone, it’s up to you where to go and what to do. The best thing about solo travel is that there are no rules — just your own intuition on the road!


In this blog post, we’ll cover the 7 most popular solo travel destinations in Southeast Asia for solo travelers so you can start planning your next adventure.


Is solo traveling a good idea?

If you have been holding back from going on a solo trip, maybe it’s time to step out of your comfort zone and give it a try. Traveling alone is for different age groups. You don’t need to take a career break or go through an expensive detox retreat to find some peace and quiet.


How to start a solo journey?

The first step into traveling is to have a well-organized trip plan before going to travel alone.


I have used GetYourGuide or Klook to distinguish book trips manage to buy tickets and accommodation from Trip.com. For my blissful experience, you can join any day trip as long as 24 hours before your action begins for a discount.


Which country is best for solo trips?

Solo travel has become an increasingly popular way to explore the world. With so many amazing destinations in the world, there is no excuse for solo travelers not to get out and see what’s around them. There’s still plenty of great places that won’t break the bank or require lots of language skills from you.


However, how do you choose where to go?


Which destination offers the most opportunities for solo travel?


To answer these questions, read on for more information about what made one of these places better than the others.


#Singapore — Best for a solo stopover

Singapore is a city with great public transport, cultural attractions, and hostels that offer solo travelers the opportunity to find themselves in this modern-day metropolis on any sunny afternoon. Singapore is one of the safest countries around the world so it is very suitable for female solo travelers.


Whether you choose to amble through Chinatown Heritage Centre or gawk at otherworldly scenes from your hotel room window overlooking Marina Bay Sands (both highly recommended). However, I would recommend you to try famous sightseeing tours around the city center with audio guides; hiking through Changi Forest Park & Botanical Garden while listening to National Parks Board present.


#Thailand, Bangkok — Best for adventure

Thailand is a country that has so much to offer for solo travelers. The culture and heritage of Thai people will make your time there unforgettable, while the bustling metropolis known as Bangkok is full of adventures just waiting to happen!


From riding elephants through rice fields or taking part in water sports on beaches along Thailand’s Andaman Sea coastlines you’ll never want to go back home again once this trip was over — not if we can help it at least…


There are so many things to see and do with vibrant nightlife scenes which you can enjoy without having to worry about your safety. The famous floating market in Damnoen Saduak is a must. My recommendation 😉


#Myanmar, Bagan — Best for culture

Bagan is Myanmar’s most popular attraction, and for good reason. You can’t miss it because there are over 2,000 beautiful stupas scattered throughout the endless countryside.


The best way to experience Bagan’s full magic is to fly on a hot air balloon at sunrise. Aside from the pagodas, you may visit one of Bagan’s local markets — such as the Mani Sithu Market — ride past the temples on a bike or e-bike, and purchase high-quality lacquer goods.


If it’s a culinary adventure you’re looking for, Bagan won’t let you down. Don’t forget to taste Burmese cuisine if you travel to Burma.
