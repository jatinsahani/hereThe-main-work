A while back, we were travelling in London.

A heavily pregnant lady got onto the bus; she was carrying parcels. There were no seats left. My husband, straight away, stood up and offered her his seat.

She was hugely offended. She proceeded to very loudly admonish him for disrespecting her. For thinking that because she was a woman, he was treating her as if she was a damsel who couldn’t stand on a bus. She was very loud and angry.


It took both of us aback. I tried to explain “This is my husband, he’s been through two pregnancies with me, puffy feet and all. In fact (my feet were again puffy- this time from air travel -aah the joys!) His only thought was, maybe you would like to sit down and put your feet up.”


She then proceeded to shout at me about sexism.


My husband quietly said to her “It’s about kindness and good manners, and nothing else. I’m not forcing you to sit in the chair -that would be sexism. Equality is about having the right to choose. You could simply have said no thank you.”


Now as I’m reading your story, I wonder what was the story behind the story for this woman. What injustices, had she faced in her life, to make her not want to accept a simple kindness from a man.


Maybe for her too, it wasn’t about the seat.
