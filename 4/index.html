Travelling the world didn’t help me figure out what I want to do with my life.

And I still haven’t become the full-fledged adult I thought I would be. What gives?

A week after my college graduation, I boarded a 14-hour flight to Paris brimming with excitement at exploring Europe for the first time.


I’m a planner.


I chose my niche, mapped out what to do every summer, played by my strengths, and almost signed a contract to accept work for a multi-national insurance company.


Ideally, I was starting my first “real world” job a few weeks after the end of my month-long trip as a management trainee. Y’know, the fancy schmancy car, 50k a month salary, and a lifetime of networking opportunities.


That was my plan.


Europe had other ideas.


In Paris, I learned to love history and literature while strolling the Latin Quarter.


Prague taught me about finding humour in tragedy whereas Vienna told me how to preserve it (whilst drinking Sachertorte).


And Rome gave me a feast that could feed a village — for a price less than that.


Finally, Barcelona kept me in touch with my Spanish roots with late-night tapa-eating & sangria-drinking sessions, court jesters, and children’s laughters…. The city made me more alive than anything before it.


I wanted to keep chasing the feeling


Safe to say, I never took the management trainee position. Despite its obvious benefits.


My whole life I was always at the back of the classroom, writing or painting, never really interacting with anyone.


Cautious by nature, I thrived in the desire to be nothing like my Mom — who loved adventures, and travelled more than any woman who had a child waiting at home.


But the strong appeal of foreign places tarnished my resolve. I found myself saving money to book trips to Cape Town, New York, Beijing, and more cities than anyone my age has ever gone.


Even when I had the opportunity to go to business school in Madrid, I couldn’t shake the feeling at how right it was for me to…well, not.


Wherever you are right now — whether you plan to build a life in a new city, or pack your bags to go, I only have one advice: chase the feeling.


Many of us go through the motions of life with ideas in our heads about who we should be, what we need to do, and where we go.


But if you surrender yourself to being flexible to the changes life has in store for you, I can promise you that it will be worth it.


Even when you’re afraid of the outcome or if you don’t have a solid roadmap yet.


Run down to the beach, take that walk, study abroad, buy a cat or a puppy or a hamster, backpack around South America, and hell, do what’s right for you.


I’ve made many mistakes. From letting anxiety overpower me or fear taunting me with my dreams.


I’ve wasted money booking flights I didn’t board. Giving up on plenty of opportunities. Tried staying sane and safe and comfortable for a few months under my Mom’s roof. I drank too much coffee — got sent to the hospital. I walked away when I shouldn’t have.


There were plenty of instances and moments lost to anger, and confusion. But I could never regret not taking that management job after college graduation.


Why? Because even if being a writer paid less, I’m always looking out for more projects during slow months, or if I eat cup noodles on various trips…


I still wouldn’t trade this life for anything.
